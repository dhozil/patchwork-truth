How to Play

💩 What is Patchwork Truth?

Patchwork Truth is a multiplayer negotiation game where you compete to build a shared truth from conflicting story fragments. Goal: weave the most coherent and fair narrative using GenLayer Intelligent Contract scoring.

This game is not about who knows the most — it is about who can build the most coherent narrative from conflicting evidence.

🎯 How It Works

1

Host Creates the Room

Host creates a room, picks a scenario, and shares the room code with other players.

2

Each Player Gets Different Fragments

16 fragments shuffled — each player gets 3 random fragments. No player has the full picture — negotiation is REQUIRED.

3

Discuss and Craft the Final Narrative

Negotiation phase: everyone submits contributions. Host weaves the best final narrative.

4

AI Evaluator Grades the Narrative

GenLayer Intelligent Contract evaluates 4 dimensions: coherence, evidence integration, argument quality, manipulation resistance.

5

Challenge Phase (Optional)

Disagree with the AI evaluation? Submit an appeal via Optimistic Democracy — community voting.

📝 How to Write a Contribution

Good contribution = specific claim + concrete evidence + narrative impact. Not an opinion without basis.

✂ Contribution Formula

Format
[MAIN CLAIM] because [EVIDENCE] — [NARRATIVE IMPACT]
Example: Mystery Scenario
"The 19:07 sensor log is the most verifiable timeline anchor because digital data is more reliable than eyewitness testimony."
Example: Corporate Scenario
"Singapore entity registered 14 Feb — 2 weeks BEFORE the board meeting — indicating advance planning, not opportunity."

⚠ What to Avoid

❌ Opinion without evidence: "In my opinion this incident is clearly not an accident"
❌ Absolutism: "This MUST be sabotage because X"
❌ Ignoring conflicting evidence: "ignore the anonymous report"
❌ Chat volume without substance: spamming without specific claims

📋 Final Narrative Tips

Template
"Based on evidence X and Y, the most coherent narrative is [THEIRS]. However there is uncertainty in [AREA] that needs to be acknowledged."

🚀 Tips and Strategy

1

Listen to Everyone

Your fragments are only 3 of 16. Other players have the evidence you need to form a complete narrative.

2

Focus on Being the Integrator

High scorers are not the loudest talkers — they are the best evidence integrators.

3

Use Evidence Language

Key words: "based on", "supported by", "consistent with", "unlike". Avoid: "in my opinion", "it seems".

4

Acknowledging Uncertainty = Strength Signal

Narratives that acknowledge evidence limitations are valued higher by the AI than baseless absolute claims.

5

Strategic Challenging

Challenge is not for attacking — it is for highlighting what matters. Correct challenge = big bonus.

Replayability: try different scenarios. Each scenario needs a different approach since the evidence types differ.
